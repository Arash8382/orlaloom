Amber glass is the secret to a moody, candle-lit table — it catches warm light and makes everything look a little vintage. Add a ribbed, fluted texture and you've got the most-pinned glassware look of the year. Here's how to build it, from everyday tumblers to a splurge coupe.

**Quick picks:** Best overall — amber ribbed tumblers · Best fluted — Megarte origami · Best centerpiece — amber pitcher · Splurge — Estelle amber coupe.

## Amber + fluted: why it works

Amber tints warm light; fluting scatters it. Together they make a glass that practically glows next to a candle — which is exactly the dark-cottagecore mood.

## Style it

Set amber tumblers and a matching pitcher on a tray with a taper candle. Mix in a clear fluted glass or two so the amber reads as a choice, not a set.

## FAQ

**Is amber glass the same as vintage 'depression glass'?**
It's the same warm look. Most amber glass sold today is new and lead-free; true depression glass is antique and sold as singles.

**Are fluted glasses hard to clean?**
The ribs can trap residue, so a bottle brush helps — but most modern fluted sets are dishwasher-safe.
