Enamelware is the original hard-wearing cottage tableware — light, nostalgic, and tougher than it looks. Whether you want the freckled splatter look or a cleaner heritage style, here are the pieces that earn a place on the table.

**Quick picks:** The icon — Crow Canyon splatter mug · Best plates — Golden Rabbit set · Doubles as a vase — Golden Rabbit pitcher · Heritage look — Falcon.

## Splatter vs. heritage

Two different moods: hand-flicked **splatterware** (Crow Canyon, Golden Rabbit) is busy, freckled, and classically Americana; **heritage** enamel (Falcon) is solid colors with a contrasting rim, calmer and more modern. Pick one lane so your shelf reads intentional.

## Care it actually needs

Enamel is fused glass over steel: durable, but a hard knock can chip it, and exposed steel can rust. Hand-wash the painted pieces, skip the microwave (it's metal), and dry promptly. Treated kindly, enamelware lasts decades — that's why so much vintage enamel survives.

## FAQ

**Is enamelware safe for the oven?**
The plates and platters here are oven-to-table safe; mugs and pitchers are not meant for direct heat. Never microwave any of it.

**Will the colors fade?**
The glaze is stable, but chips are the real enemy — store pieces so they aren't clattering against each other.
